Timeline of Mustafa Kemal Ataturk's Life:


  • 1881 - Born in Salonika (now Thessaloniki, Greece), and was given the name of "Mustafa."
  • 1886 - His father Ali Riza died.
  • 1895 - Graduated from the military secondary school where he was given the name of "Kemal," meaning The Perfect One, from his mathematics teacher. Then he progressed to the military school in Monastir (now Bitola, North Macedonia).
  • 1899 - Completed his education at Monastir, and entered the War College in Istanbul in March.
  • 1902 - Graduated from the War College as a second lieutenant and ranking in the top 10 of his class of more than 450 students. Then, entered the General Staff College.
  • 1905 - Graduated from the General Staff College as a staff captain and ranking fifth out of a class of 57, and became one of the empire's leading young officers.
  • 1907 - Promoted to lieutenant commander and with his trusted friends he created "Committee of Union and Progress" (CUP).
  • 1911 - Promoted to major rank and in 1911-1912 there was a war between Turks and Italians known as "Battle of Tripolitania". There he was the head of the various forces.
  • 1912-1913 - Balkan Wars were between balkanic countries (Serbia, Greece, Bulgaria) and Ottoman Empire. In the first balkan war the Ottoman empire lost majority of Balkan lands. The second balkan war was between Bulgaria and other Balkan countries as a result the power dynamics has changed and Ottoman Empire lost its power in the Balkans.
  • 1914 - After Balkan Wars, relations were renewed with Bulgaria and he assigned his former schoolmate as ambassador,then got promoted to lieutenant colonel.
  • 1914-1918 - First World War: There were two major alliances, the Allies (France, UK, Russia, and USA) and the Central Powers (Germany, Austria-Hungary, the Ottoman Empire).
  • 1915 - During the battle, Mustafa Kemal was hit by a piece of shrapnel, which lodged in the watch he carried in his breast pocket and thus failed to cause him serious injury. His success at Gallipoli thrust Mustafa Kemal onto the world scene. He was hailed as the “Saviour of Istanbul” and was promoted to colonel.
  • 1916 - Mustafa Kemal was assigned to the Russian front and promoted to brigadier general, acquiring the title of pasha. He was the only Turkish general to win any victories over the Russians on the Eastern Front. Later that year, he took over the command of the Second Army in southeastern Anatolia.
  • 1919 - Turkish War of Independence started with Mustafa Kemal Ataturk starting his journey to Samsun in 19 May 1919, and concluded with win in 30 August 1922. He abandoned his official reason for being in Anatolia, which was to restore order, he headed inland for Amasya, there he told a cheering crowd that the sultan was the prisoner of the Allies and that he had come to prevent the nation from slipping through the fingers of its people. This became his message to the Turks of Anatolia. The Allies pressured the sultan to recall Mustafa Kemal, who ignored all communications from Istanbul. Imperial orders for his arrest were circulated. Mustafa Kemal avoided dismissal from the army by officially resigning late on the evening of July 7.
  • 1920 - In June, the Allies handed the sultan the Treaty of Sevres, with this the size of Ottoman Empire was reduced, with Greece one of the major beneficiaries. Armenia was declared independent.
  • 1921 - As the war against the Greeks started to go well for Mustafa Kemal’s forces, France and Italy negotiated with the nationalist government in Ankara. They withdrew their troops from Anatolia. This left the Armenians in southeastern Anatolia without the protection of the French troops. Armenian Socialist Republic surrendered early in November 1920. The Greeks did not resume their offensive until March 1921, then launched another offensive on July 13, 1921. Mustafa Kemal Ataturk defeated Greeks at the Battle of Sakarya and initiated an offensive that pushed the Greeks to the sea at Izmir. He got promoted to commander-in-chief in August then after the win of Battle of Sakarya Square, he gets promoted to marshal which is the highest rank in the military.
  • 1922 - With Anatolia rid of most of the Allies, the GNA, at the behest of Mustafa Kemal, voted on November 1, 1922, to abolish the sultanate. This was soon followed by the flight into exile of Sultan Mehmed VI on November 17.
  • 1923 - The Allies then invited the Ankara government to discussions that resulted in the signing of the Treaty of Lausanne on July 24, 1923. This treaty fixed the European border of Turkey at the Maritsa River in eastern Thrace. The nationalists occupied Istanbul on October 2. Ankara was named the capital, and on October 29 the Turkish republic was proclaimed.
  • 1924 - In March 3, 1924 the caliphate was abolished, abolition of the religious courts followed on April 8.
  • 1928 - The arabic script in which the Ottoman Turkish language had been written for centuries was replaced with the new Turkish Alphabet.
  • 1934 - Women were given the vote for parliamentary members and were made eligible to hold parliamentary seats. The adoption of surnames or family name was another important step.
  • 1938 - His illness, cirrhosis of the liver, was diagnosed too late. In November 10, 1938, he died at 9:05 AM in Dolmabahce Palace in Istanbul.
